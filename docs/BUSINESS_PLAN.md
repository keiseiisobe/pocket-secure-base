# DeepBreath

### Identity
DeepBreath is a mobile exploration partner (the **"Navigator Whale"**) that provides high-quality mobile reliability for neurodivergent individuals navigating unpredictable urban environments.

### Problem
Neurodivergent individuals face high "sensory friction" (noise, crowds, unpredictability) in the outside world. Traditional navigation tools optimize for speed, ignoring the sensory triggers that cause acute anxiety and social isolation, even for those with a primary secure base at home.

### Our solution
A digital "Navigator Whale" that provides absolute predictability and safety. By using real-time transit data and on-device AI, DeepBreath absorbs environmental turbulence, allowing users to explore the world with the same confidence they feel at home.

### Target market
The primary audience is neurodivergent individuals (ASD, ADHD, HSP/SPS). The secondary market includes caregivers, social workers, and welfare organizations who provide the primary secure base.

### The competition
Standard navigation apps like Google and Apple Maps optimize for speed rather than sensory health. While niche accessibility maps exist, they are often static and lack the real-time, emotive, and offline-reliable support provided by DeepBreath.

### Revenue streams
DeepBreath utilizes a Freemium model for direct users, bulk B2B licensing for social welfare organizations and schools, and B2G accessibility fees from municipalities and transit authorities.

### Marketing activities
DeepBreath will communicate with users through institutional referrals from social workers, strategic partnerships with transit authorities (e.g., Tokyo Metro), and community-driven sensory mapping networks.

### Expenses
*   Cloud AI API tokens (Gemini 2.0 & Google Search).
*   High-fidelity, human-voiced audio asset production.
*   R&D for NPU-optimized local AI models and low-latency orchestration.

### Team and key roles
The project is led by a specialized team of software engineers and welfare experts. Key roles focus on AI orchestration, sensory data curation, and building strategic partnerships with the public and welfare sectors.

### Milestones
As the service grows, DeepBreath will launch targeted pilots in major urban centers—starting with Greater Tokyo—to validate the "Navigator Whale" model as a high-impact tool for social accessibility.
