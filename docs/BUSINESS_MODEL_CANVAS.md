# Business Model Canvas: DeepBreath

This document outlines the business model for **DeepBreath**, the **Mobile Exploration Partner** (the **"Navigator Whale"**) designed for **neurodivergent individuals**.

## 1. Customer Segments
*   **Primary (B2C):** neurodivergent individuals (ASD, ADHD, HSP/SPS) who face sensory challenges in urban environments.
*   **Secondary (Beneficiaries):** Caregivers, parents, and guardians seeking increased autonomy for their loved ones.
*   **Institutional (B2B):** 
    *   **Social Welfare Organizations:** NGOs and support centers providing the primary human-created Secure Base.
    *   **Special Education Schools:** Institutions preparing students for independent life.
    *   **Corporations:** DEI (Diversity, Equity, and Inclusion) programs supporting neurodivergent employees.
*   **Public Sector (B2G):** Municipalities and transit authorities (e.g., Tokyo Metro) aiming to improve urban accessibility.

## 2. Value Propositions
*   **Mental Health Navigation:** Optimization of routes based on "Sensory Health" (avoiding noise and crowds) rather than just speed.
*   **Mobile Reliability:** A digital companion that provides predictability and safety during excursions from the primary base.
*   **The Navigator Whale (Offline Safety):** Immediate, zero-latency grounding and guidance via local AI, even in subway "dead zones."
*   **Sensory Friction Mitigation:** Mitigation of environmental unpredictability through LLM-driven "pre-scans" of routes (construction, events).
*   **Empowered Autonomy:** Empowering users to explore the city confidently, reducing isolation.

## 3. Channels
*   **Mobile App Stores:** Direct distribution (iOS/Android).
*   **Institutional Referral:** Social workers and educators recommending the tool to clients/students.
*   **Strategic Partnerships:** Integration with transit authority apps or municipal accessibility portals.
*   **Community Networks:** Support groups for ASD/ADHD and HSP forums.

## 4. Customer Relationships
*   **Empathetic Companion:** An LLM-driven "Situational Advisor" providing calm, senior social worker-style guidance.
*   **Crowdsourced Trust:** User-verified sensory tags ("Safe Havens") creating a community-driven safety map.
*   **Privacy-First:** Strict "No Data Selling" policy to maintain trust with a vulnerable population.
*   **Predictability:** Consistent, reliable response from the Local Reflex Engine in crisis moments.

## 5. Revenue Streams
*   **B2C Freemium:** Free basic navigation; "Guardian Pass" subscription for Offline AI, advanced noise monitoring, and saved Safe Havens.
*   **B2B2C Licensing:** Bulk licenses sold to NGOs, schools, and corporate HR departments.
*   **B2G Accessibility Fees:** Municipal or transit authority subsidies to provide the service free to residents/passengers.
*   **Insurance/Healthcare:** Risk mitigation partnerships with health providers to reduce anxiety-related incidents.

## 6. Key Activities
*   **AI Orchestration:** Managing the hybrid Cloud/Edge AI lifecycle (Gemini + Local Reflex Engine).
*   **Sensory Mapping:** Continuous curation and verification of environmental metadata (noise, light, crowds).
*   **Partnership Management:** Building relationships with transit authorities and welfare institutions.
*   **Asset Creation:** Developing high-fidelity, human-voiced audio grounding libraries.
*   **App Development:** Maintenance of the Flutter-based "Secure Base" platform.

## 7. Key Resources
*   **AI Models:** Gemini 2.0 Flash (Strategic Planner), Local Reflex Engine (The Guardian).
*   **Proprietary Sensory Map:** Database of quiet zones and sensory hotspots.
*   **Vocabulary Asset Store:** Library of human-voiced social worker interventions.
*   **Real-time Data Hooks:** GTFS-RT (Transit) and Google Search (Grounding) API integrations.
*   **Technical Infrastructure:** Cloud backend for situational reasoning and TTS streaming.

## 8. Key Partners
*   **AI Providers:** Google (Gemini/Search) and ElevenLabs (Emotive TTS).
*   **Data Providers:** Tokyo Metro (ODPT) and OpenStreetMap.
*   **Welfare Sector:** Japanese Association of Certified Social Workers and regional NGOs.
*   **OS Platforms:** Google (Android/AICore) and Apple (iOS/CoreML) for hardware-accelerated AI.

## 9. Cost Structure
*   **Variable (API) Costs:** Cloud LLM tokens (Gemini) and Google Search API requests (~$37.50/day per kDAU).
*   **Cloud Infrastructure:** Serverless compute, database hosting, and TTS streaming.
*   **R&D:** Development of NPU-optimized classifiers and low-latency orchestration logic.
*   **Content Production:** Recording human-voiced interventions and "Social Story" assets.
*   **B2B Sales/Marketing:** Operational costs for institutional and municipal relationship building.
