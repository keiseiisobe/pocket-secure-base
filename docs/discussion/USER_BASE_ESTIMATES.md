# User Base Estimates: Market Potential

To estimate the number of potential users for **DeepBreath**, we analyze three distinct "User Tiers" ranging from core neurodivergent individuals to the broader population with sensory sensitivities.

### 1. Tier 1: The Core Target (Diagnosed ASD & ADHD)
This tier represents individuals who face significant executive function and sensory challenges daily.
*   **Japan Statistics**: The Japanese Ministry of Health, Labour and Welfare (MHLW) reported in 2022 that approximately **4.3% of the population** has a diagnosed developmental disability (Hattatsu Shōgai) [1].
*   **Tokyo Focus**: In the Greater Tokyo Area (Population: ~38 million), this accounts for approximately **1.6 million potential users**.
*   **Global Scale**: Globally, the CDC estimates 1 in 36 children (2.8%) have ASD, and adult ADHD prevalence is estimated at 2.5–4.4% [2][3].

### 2. Tier 2: The Extended Target (HSP & Sensory Sensitive)
This tier includes "Highly Sensitive People" (HSP) and those with Sensory Processing Sensitivity (SPS) who do not have a formal disability diagnosis but experience high stress in urban environments.
*   **Prevalence**: Research indicates that **15% to 20% of the general population** possesses high sensory sensitivity traits [4].
*   **Japan Potential**: This expands the potential user base in Japan to roughly **18–25 million people**.

### 3. Tier 3: Secondary Beneficiaries (Caregivers & Social Workers)
Our app also serves the "Primary Secure Base"—the people who support Tier 1.
*   **Social Workers in Japan**: As of 2023, there are over **250,000 Certified Social Workers** in Japan [5].
*   **Role**: These professionals are key "Decision Influencers" who would recommend the app to their clients as a life-skills tool for independent travel.

### Strategic Insight
The initial market for **DeepBreath** should focus on the **1.6 million residents in Greater Tokyo** who navigate the world's most complex transit system (Tokyo Metro) while managing developmental challenges. Capturing even 1% of this core market (**16,000 users**) would validate the project as a high-impact social welfare tool.
