# Future Service Ideas: Pocket Secure Base

This document serves as a repository for potential features and services to be integrated into the "Pocket Secure Base" ecosystem to further support independence and reduce anxiety.

## 1. Communication & Sensory Management
- **Emergency AAC (Augmentative and Alternative Communication)**: Simplified visual cards or "Quick-Speak" buttons for users who may become non-verbal or lose the ability to articulate needs during a sensory shutdown (e.g., "I need a quiet space," "Please don't touch me").
- **Ambient Noise Monitor**: A proactive tool that uses the device microphone to alert the user if the decibel levels in their current environment are rising to a point that typically triggers their stress.

## 2. Transition & Routine Support
- **Visual Transition Timers**: For "One-Tap Home" or stressless routes, adding visual countdowns or "step-by-step" progress bars to reduce the uncertainty of travel time.
- **Social Story Integration**: Small, swipeable "Social Stories" or checklists for common but stressful public interactions (e.g., "How to order at a cafe" or "What to do if the bus is late") integrated directly into the navigation flow.
- **Voice-Guided Safe Return**: A calm, familiar voice (e.g., a recorded loved one) providing simple, non-directional encouragement while haptics lead the way, reducing cognitive load during high-stress "panic" moments.

## 3. Community-Driven Insights
- **User-Verified Sensory Tags**: Allow users to tag "Safe Havens" with specific sensory details, such as "Dim lighting," "No background music," or "Quiet staff," creating a more granular search than just a general "quiet" label.
- **Crowdsourced "Current Calm" Status**: A simple "Thumbs Up/Down" from users currently at a Safe Haven to indicate if the real-time environment matches its usual quiet reputation.
- **SNS Trend Analysis**: Monitoring real-time social media trends for sudden crowd gatherings, protests, or noise events to provide immediate re-routing.
- **High-Resolution Human Flow (Agoop)**: Integration of population density heatmaps to provide more granular, block-by-block crowd avoidance.

## 4. Post-Episode Support
- **Recovery Mode**: After a Panic Button event is resolved, the app could enter a "Low Stimulation" mode with a simplified UI and a "Cool Down" timer, suggesting the user find a nearby Safe Haven to rest before continuing their journey.

## 5. LLM-Powered Personalization & Safety Net
- **Contextual Social Interpreter**: An LLM-driven tool that analyzes ambiguous social situations (via text or voice-to-text) to explain hidden social cues, subtext, or the likely emotional state of others.
- **On-the-Fly Social Scripting**: Generates custom step-by-step "Social Scripts" for specific interactions based on GPS location and intent (e.g., "How to report a lost item at this specific train station").
- **Review-Based Sensory Analysis**: Scans public reviews for a location (cafes, malls, parks) to extract specific sensory metadata like "echoey," "uses strong incense," or "unexpectedly crowded on Tuesdays."
- **Empathetic Grounding Companion**: A specialized LLM chatbot trained in grounding techniques (CBT/DBT) that provides personalized, non-judgmental conversation during a sensory overload or panic episode.
- **Simplified "Easy Read" Summarizer**: Automatically transforms complex public information—like transit delay announcements, store policies, or maps—into high-clarity, bulleted "Easy Read" formats in real-time.

## 6. Wearable Integration
- **Discreet Haptic Navigation**: Using Apple Watch or Wear OS to provide directional "nudges" (vibration patterns) so the user doesn't have to look at their phone.
- **Physical SOS Trigger**: A dedicated watch complication or button sequence to trigger "Secure Base" mode instantly and discreetly.
- **Bio-Feedback Sync**: Using watch heart-rate data to automatically trigger grounding exercises if the user's stress levels (heart rate variability) spike.
